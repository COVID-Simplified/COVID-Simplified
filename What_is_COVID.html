What is COVID-19?




About The Virus

COVID stands for, "Coronavirus Disease." It is a type of virus. A virus is a microscopic organism that essentially reproduces by taking over your cell. It is a respiratory virus. This means that it affects your lungs and other parts of your body involved with breathing. This virus spreads through droplets in the air, mainly from coughing or sneezing. You will learn more about this in the symptoms section.



How it all began

On December 31, 2019, the World Health Organization(WHO) said that a mysterious disease was sickening dozens of people in China. Also, many of the people who were sick had visited an animal market in Wuhan, China. The first coronavirus death was in China on January 11, 2020. This was a 61-year-old man who had visited that very same market. The first coronavirus case was identified in the US 10 days after. It was a man from Washington state who had recently been to Wuhan. On January 30, 2020, WHO declared a global health emergency. The first COVID-19 death in the US was on February 6 in Santa Clara, California. Then, on March 13, 2020, President Trump declared a national emergency. The total number of coronavirus cases in the world hit 1 million on April 2, 2020.



How it spreads

There are a few ways that COVID-19 can spread. You can be safer by understanding these.


  • The virus can spread through the air through actions such as sneezing and coughing.
  • It can through contact with other people who have COVID. Some examples of this would be shaking hands or hugging an infected person.
  • Lastly, the virus can spread when touching something that has the virus. Suppose someone who had COVID touched an object, such as a door handle. If someone else touched that same object, the virus could have been passed on.

Inform family and friends about the way the virus spreads so that everyone can stay safe and healthy.



Symptoms

There are many symptoms of COVID-19. Knowing these symptoms will help keep you and others safe.

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Trouble Breathing
  • Headache
  • Tiredness
  • Vomiting
  • Diarrhea
  • Runny Nose
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore Throat

There are also a few severe symptoms. If you or someone in your family has one or more of the symptoms listed below, contact a doctor and get medical attention immediately.

  • Pain in chest
  • Much trouble breathing
  • Bluish face
  • Can't stay awake
  • Confusion

If you or someone in your family has one of the symptoms listed above, contact a doctor and get medical attention immediately. It is important to know and recognize these symptoms. Remember to stay safe and healthy during these tough times.



People at higher risk for COVID-19

Some people are at a higher risk of developing severe symptoms for COVID-19 than others. If you fall under one or more of the categories below, make sure to follow the guidelines listed extremely carefully(as should everyone).


  • 65 or older
    • The risk of developing severe symptoms increases with age. People 85 and older are at the highest risk. Also, in the U.S., about 80% of deaths from the virus have been people 65 or older.

  • Lung Problems
    • COVID-19 attacks and weakens your lungs. If you have lung problems such as asthma, you are more likely to develop severe symptoms.

  • Heart disease, diabetes, obesity, liver disease, etc.
    • Any diseases that weaken your immune system may give you a higher chance to get the virus and develop severe symptoms.
