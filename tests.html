Tests




Who should get tested


  • People who have symptoms of COVID-19.
  • People who have been in close contact with someone who has tested positive for COVID-19.
    • Close contact is defined as being within six feet of a person who tested positive for a total of 15 minutes.
  • People who have been in a situation that has a higher-risk for COVID-19, such as traveling or a mass gathering.

What to do if exposed


  • These steps should be followed if you have been exposed to:
    • Someone who has symptoms of COVID-19
    • Someone who has tested positive for COVID-19
  • After being exposed, you should stay at home for at least 14 days and socially distance yourself from everyone, including your family, at all times.
  • While you are staying away from others:
    • Check your temperature two times a day
    • Check for symptoms
    • Avoid contact with people who have a higher risk of severe symptoms from COVID-19.
    • Contact a doctor to arrange a test.
    • Self-quarantine while waiting for results from a test.

What to do if tested positive


There are many things you should do if you test positive for COVID-19. These steps are recommended by the CDC(Center for Disease Control and Prevention) and will help keep you and others safe. Some of these are to:


  • Stay home unless receiving medical attention. This is important because it will limit other people's exposure to the virus. Do not go to public areas including public transportation.
  • Stay in one room to keep you away from other people and pets.
  • Use a separate bathroom from other family members.
  • Wear a mask at all times when around other people.
  • Constantly monitor your symptoms.
