Preventative Measures




Wear a Mask



  • Which mask to use?

    You should wear a mask that first properly and make sure that it always covers your nose and mouth. A mask will be extremely helpful in keeping you and others safe. Always make sure that your mask completely covers your nose and mouth. This is VERY important for safety. Otherwise, your mask will not work properly.


  • How to wash

    Wash your mask whenever it is used. If your mask is disposable, throw it away after wearing it once.


  • When not to wear a mask

    • If you are in a restaurant, only remove your mask while eating and remain at least 6 feet away from other diners.
    • When swimming, don't wear a mask. This is because a wet mask can quickly become difficult to breathe in and may not work as well.
    • If you are exercising or doing something that requires a lot of breathing, choose an open space. The outdoors is a good open area to use. Also, try to stay at least 6 feet away from other people at all times.

    Keep in mind that you will have a higher chance of getting the virus if you are not wearing a mask or social distancing when around other people.


  • Warnings and Additional Info

    Always wear a mask when around people who don't live in your household if you are of age two or older. Keep in mind that wearing a mask does not mean that you don’t have to social distance. Always do both when outside. Additionally, wash your hands properly after touching or removing your mask




Social Distancing


Social distancing is staying 6 feet away from other people. While this does not mean that you don’t have to wear a mask, social distancing from other people can help prevent the spread of COVID-19. Staying away from other people can stop the number of droplets that land in the mouth or noses of people nearby.



Using Tissues


Since COVID-19 spreads through droplets in the air, sneezing or coughing can spread this virus to other people. Use a tissue to block these droplets and make sure to throw it away properly afterward. Also, make sure to wash your hands after using a tissue.



Don't touch your face


It is important to try not to touch your face when outside or even at home. The germs on your hands can end up on your face and increase the chance that you get sick. If you need to touch your face, make sure to wash your hands properly before doing so.



Stay home if sick


If you are sick, stay at home so that you don't spread your sickness to others. This is crucial to stopping the spread of the virus. Even if you have the flu or something else, stay home. You could get other people sick, and it becomes serious, they would add to the number of people in the hospitals. However, if you are really not well and need to go to the hospital or receive other kinds of help, it is ok to leave your home. Just make sure to follow all of the preventive measures listed on this page. It is generally recommended to stay at home unless necessary.



Cleaning your hands


Washing your hands is very important for staying safe. Your hands gather germs throughout the day, so washing your hands often is extremely important. A good, effective way to wash your hands is to use water, soap, and then more water to wash the soap off. When using soap, you should scrub your entire hand. This includes the back of your hand, in-between your fingers, and their tips. When you are scrubbing your hands with soap, you should sing the Happy Birthday song twice before washing the soap off your hands with water. This will kill most of the germs on your hand and you can stay healthy and safe! When soap and water are not available you should use hand sanitizer. This is slightly less effective than soap and water, but it can be an easy and fast alternative. Before using the hand sanitizer, check if the bottle has at least 60% alcohol. If it is less than 60%, the hand sanitizer may not be as effective.



Disinfect


Clean surfaces or objects that are frequently touched. This includes electronics such as a phone or tablet. Make sure to use a proper disinfectant and follow the instructions. For example, some disinfectants need to be left on the surface for a minute to be fully effective. If someone has the virus and touches one of these objects, the next person to touch it could get the virus.



When to travel


Only travel if necessary so that you limit the spread of COVID-19. If you are going to meet someone, make sure that they are not at increased risk of catching the virus. Do not go to places where many people will be close together such as weddings, funerals, parties, concerts, or parades.
